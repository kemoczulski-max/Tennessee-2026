02
Big Day
Fri · Oct 16

Alum Cave · Cades Cove · Blackberry Farm

6:00 AM

Alum Cave Trail → Bluffs

Parking gone by 8am — go early. Gradual climb through old-growth hardwood, Arch Rock at 1.3mi, then the bluffs. Turn around at the bluffs for manageable morning hike before Cades Cove.

📍 4.6mi RT ⬆ 1,200ft ⏱ ~3hrs
Late AM

Drive to Cades Cove

~45min from Alum Cave. One-way 11-mile loop through the most photographed valley in the park. Historic cabins, churches, grist mill. Peak foliage in October meadow.

Noon

Cades Cove Loop + Abrams Falls

Drive the full loop then hike Abrams Falls — highest water volume waterfall in the park, 20ft drop. Wildlife very active in October: black bear, deer, turkey frequent the cove.

🚗 11mi loop 📍 Abrams Falls 5mi RT ⬆ 340ft
Evening

Dinner · The Barn at Blackberry Farm

Relais & Châteaux. James Beard Award-winning. Farm-to-table multi-course, 9,000-label wine cellar, 300+ whiskies. Outside guest reservation required. blackberryconcierge@blackberryfarm.com · 865-380-2086. Use Emma's uncle contact. Book now.

🍷 Outside Guest Reservation Required37min drive

Total hiking Day 2: ~10mi · ~1,500ft elevation gain

03
Summit

Dome · Helicopter · Coaster · Dinner

Morning

Kuwohi (Clingmans Dome)

Highest point in the park at 6,643ft. Paved trail to observation tower — short but steep. 360° panoramic views of fall color across multiple ridges. Go early before Saturday crowds.

📍 1mi RT ⬆ 330ft 🏔 6,643ft
Midday

Grand Tour Helicopter · Scenic Helicopter Tours

90-mile flight over the park: Sevierville → Pigeon Forge → Gatlinburg → Cades Cove → Sugarlands → Tremont. Most complete aerial view of the Smokies available. FAA/NPS approved to fly inside the national park. Peak foliage from the air in mid-October.

🚁 90mi · scenichelicoptertours.com
Afternoon

Rail Runner Coaster · Anakeesta, Gatlinburg

Fastest mountain coaster in Gatlinburg at ~27mph through dense fall foliage. Downtown location, easy walk from parking. Hand-brake controlled speed.

~27mphDowntown Gatlinburg

Classic

Riverside on LeConte Creek. Classic steakhouse, salad bar from a canoe. Gatlinburg institution since the 70s.

Mountain Lodge

More elevated, newer concept. Check OpenTable for current menu and Oct 17 availability.

Activities

Adventures

Day 2 · 6am

Alum Cave Trail

Best single trail in the Smokies. Old-growth hardwood, creek crossings, Arch Rock tunnel, then the bluffs. Go before 8am — parking is gone by then in October.

4.6mi RT 1,200ft gain ~3hrs Moderate/Strenuous

Day 2 · Afternoon

Cades Cove Loop

11-mile one-way scenic drive. Most photographed valley in the park. Historic buildings, peak foliage meadows, wildlife. Add Abrams Falls (5mi RT) — highest-volume waterfall in park.

11mi loop drive Abrams Falls 5mi RT 340ft gain

Day 3 · Morning

Kuwohi / Clingmans Dome

Highest point in the Smokies at 6,643ft. Paved summit trail to observation tower. 360° views of peak fall color across multiple mountain ridges. Short but steep paved path.

1mi RT 330ft gain 6,643ft elevation

Day 3 · Midday

Grand Tour Helicopter

90-mile scenic flight over the park. Sevierville → Gatlinburg → into the national park boundary → Cades Cove. Peak foliage from above in mid-October. FAA/NPS approved flights inside park.

🚁 90mi flight scenichelicoptertours.com

Day 3 · Afternoon

Rail Runner · Anakeesta

Fastest mountain coaster in Gatlinburg (~27mph) through dense fall canopy. Downtown location, hand-brake controlled speed. Easy add-on before dinner.

~27mph Downtown Gatlinburg
